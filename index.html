The Problem

When data centers orbit the Earth,
CDN assumptions break.

Today's CDNs assume fixed geography. Orbital compute shatters every premise. There is no tool that lets an engineer reason about, route over, or visualize this system. We build that tool.

⚡

No Fixed Hierarchy

PoPs move at 7.5 km/s. Traditional CDN placement algorithms assume static geography.

☀️

Intermittent Power

A satellite enters Earth's shadow every ~90 min orbit. Power is finite without sunlight. Routing to an eclipsed DC drains its battery.

☢️

Radiation Exposure

The South Atlantic Anomaly causes bit-flips in hardware. No CDN today reasons about radiation zones.

🌧️

Weather-Dependent Last Mile

Clouds block optical ground links. Rain causes RF fade. The egress gateway matters — and changes.

🕸️

Topology Mutates Every Second

ISLs appear and vanish as satellites move. Static shortest paths are wrong by the time they're computed.

Physics That Drive Design

Every routing decision is
physics-grounded.

🔬 Vacuum Beats Glass

c / 1.47

Light in silica fiber travels at c/1.47 ≈ 204,000 km/s. In vacuum it's 299,792 km/s. Laser ISLs are 40–47% faster per km. For any path > ~3,000 km, LEO+ISL can beat fiber on latency. This is the core value proposition.

🛰️ Topology Changes Every Second

~4 ISLs

A Walker-Delta constellation gives each satellite 2 intra-plane + 2 cross-plane ISLs. Cross-plane links destabilize near poles (relative angular velocity spikes). A static shortest path is wrong — hence the GNN that generalizes over changing topology.

🌑 Eclipse Is Real & Periodic

~90 min

LEO satellites cycle in and out of Earth's shadow every orbit. In eclipse, a satellite drains a finite battery — solar power goes to zero. Routing compute to a sunlit DC is genuinely cheaper, which is why Suncatcher chose dawn–dusk SSO.

☢️ South Atlantic Anomaly

SAA

The inner Van Allen belt dips low over the South Atlantic. Particle flux there raises Single-Event Upset (bit-flip) and Total Ionizing Dose rates. Google's Suncatcher tests confirmed HBM is most sensitive. We route sensitive compute away from SAA-transiting nodes.

🌧️ Ground Links Are Weather-Exposed

Rain Fade

Ka-band RF suffers rain fade. Optical ground links are blocked by clouds. The classic mitigation: site diversity — pick a different, clear-sky gateway. This is the "weather at ground station" objective, and it changes the egress point in real time.

📐 Geodesic RTT Is the Benchmark

85–117 ms

Hypatia showed real LEO RTTs sit close to great-circle RTT but with time-varying jitter as satellites move and links hand over (e.g., London–Paris range). Our latency model is dynamic, not static, and validated against Hypatia figures within ~10%.

The Core Contribution

Six-objective
cost model.

Latency-only routing is solved and boring. No production CDN today reasons about solar exposure or SAA transit — because no production CDN is in orbit. We built the cost model that will define this category.

⚡

Latency

Propagation + transmission + queuing delay per edge. Vacuum speed on ISL, c/1.47 on fiber legs. Validated against Hypatia benchmarks.

d/v + d/C + q
🔋

Energy

Per-bit optical link energy × distance + fixed pointing/slew cost for activating a laser terminal. Penalizes unnecessary long hops.

e_link · d + e_point
📊

Congestion

M/M/1-style penalty on link utilization ρ. Blows up as a link saturates → load balancing falls out naturally from the optimizer.

ρ / (1 − ρ)
☢️

Radiation

Penalty for routing through a node currently inside the South Atlantic Anomaly or polar horn. Severity scales with live Kp index.

𝟙[SAA] · severity(Kp)
☀️

Solar / Eclipse State

Penalize using an eclipsed node, more severely as battery depletes. Eclipse computed from sun vector vs. Earth's umbra geometry.

𝟙[eclipse] · (1 − SoC)
🌧️

Weather (Gateway)

Rain rate for RF gateways + cloud cover for optical downlinks. Drives automatic site diversity — the system picks a clear-sky egress.

α·rain + β·cloud

Three Core Decisions

Every request, three
real-time choices.

01

DC Selection

Which orbital data center should process this request? Evaluate candidate DCs by uplink+ISL latency, solar state (sunlit wins unless latency strongly disagrees), radiation exposure, and current queue load.

argmin: latency_to_DC + w_sol·S_DC + w_rad·R_DC + queue_DC
02

Path Selection

When to route via laser ISL vs. drop to ground? Multi-objective Dijkstra minimizes the full cost function. A pure-ISL path wins when it's >~3,000 km (vacuum advantage) and the mesh isn't congested. Emergent behavior, not a hand-coded rule.

ISL vs. ground: falls out of Cost(P,…) minimization
03

Gateway Selection

Which ground station handles the egress? argmin over visible gateways of downlink latency + weather penalty. Rain or cloud at the nearest gateway automatically pushes egress to a farther but clear one — site diversity demonstrated live.

argmin: downlink_latency + w_wx·W_gateway

The Genuine Novelty

What makes this
research-grade.

A routing policy that trades latency against energy, solar availability, orbital congestion, radiation exposure, and ground-station weather — and a GNN+DRL controller that learns this trade-off on a constellation graph that mutates every second.

🔬 Why GNN+DRL and Not Just Dijkstra?

The graph mutates every second. Links appear/vanish, DCs move in/out of sun/SAA. A GNN produces node/edge embeddings that are permutation- and size-invariant, so a policy trained on the constellation generalizes to unseen snapshots without retraining.

📈 Proven Research Lineage

GRouting (GNN+DRL for LEO), GRLR (GNN+Actor-Critic for mega-LEO), and GDRL-SFCR (~11% lower delay, ~14% lower load vs. heuristics) all do GNN+DRL but only for latency+load. We extend to a 6-objective, physics-grounded setting. That extension is novel.

🏆 Pareto Front, Not a Single Metric

We expose the full trade-off surface: k-shortest-paths + non-dominated filtering → Pareto front of latency vs. energy vs. reliability. Flipping to "green" policy demonstrably raises % solar-served and avoids SAA transits at a quantified latency cost.

✅ Honest About Assumptions

We simulate the optimistic future and are explicit that cost weights encode assumptions. Launch economics, thermal management without convection, and SEE-safe training are still research open questions. Judges respect honesty more than overpromising.
